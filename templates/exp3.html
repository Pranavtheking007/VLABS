Experiment 3 - Binary Frequency Shift Keying

Aim Apparatus Theory Block Diagram Procedure Conclusion Simulation

Aim: -

To Observe Modulation and Demodulation of Binary Frequency Shift Keying


Apparatus: -

Data Conditioning and Carrier Modulation Transmitter Trainer Kit, CRO, Patchcords.


Theory: -

Carrier modulation is a technique by which digital data is made to modulate a continuous wavecarrier. For all type of carrier modulation the carrier frequency should be at least 2 times that of modulating frequency to satisfy the Nyquist criterion (sampling Theorem).

Frequency Shift Keying Modulation Techniques: In frequency shift keying modulation techniques, the modulated output shifts between two frequencies for all ‘one’ to ‘zero’ transitions. The carrier frequency chosen for FSK modulation are 2MHz and 1MHz.Note that the above frequencies are greater than twice the modulating frequency.

    I. Input data

    II. Carrier Frequency

    III. Modulated signal at Modulated Output.


Conclusion: -

FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) is a digital modulation technique that involves changing the frequency of the carrier wave to transmit digital data. FSK is easy to implement and is resistant to noise and interference, making it suitable for short-distance communication. It also has the advantage of allowing multiple signals to be transmitted simultaneously. However, FSK is not suitable for long-distance communication due to its narrow bandwidth, and it requires precise tuning of the receiver to distinguish between the two frequencies. Overall, FSK is a simple and reliable modulation technique that is widely used in applications such as wireless communication, radio broadcasting, and remote controls.
