Experiment 4 - Binary Phase Shift Keying

Aim Apparatus Theory Block Diagram Procedure Conclusion Simulation

Aim: -

To Observe Modulation and Demodulation of Binary Phase Shift Keying


Apparatus: -

BPSK Modulation/Demodulation Trainer Kit, CRO,Patchcords.


Theory: -

Carrier modulation is a technique by which digital data is made to modulate a continuous wavecarrier. For all type of carrier modulation the carrier frequency should be at least 2 times that of modulating frequency to satisfy the Nyquist criterion (sampling Theorem).



Block Diagram: -

Phase Shift Keying Modulation Techniques:

In phase shift keying modulation techniques , the modulated output switches between in-phase and out of phase component of the carrier frequency for all ‘one’ to ‘zero’ transitions.The carrier frequency chosen for PSK modulation are 1MHz (0 Degree) and 1Mhz(180 Degree).


Procedure: -

  1. Connect AC supply to the kit.
  2. Make connections as per the Circuit Diagram.
  3. Switch on the power
  4. Observe the following waveforms on CRO:

    I. Input data

    II. Carrier Frequency

    III. Modulated signal at Modulated Output.


Conclusion: -

Thus In BPSK signal the carrier is directly phased modulated, that is, the phase of the carrier is shifted by the incoming binary data. In the generation of BPSK signal the carrier frequency is phase-shifted 180 degree.
